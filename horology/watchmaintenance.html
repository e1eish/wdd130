How to Maintain a Mechanical Watch - An Overview

Another part of horology is working on watches. If you want to know the basics of watch maintenance then this the right place.

The First Step

 First you must open the watch's case. Watches have many different ways of opening but many wrist watches open by either prying off the back of the case or screwing the back off. Now, to get the mechanism and dial out you have to take out the crown.

The Second Step

 Now that you have the mechanism out of the case you can take off the dial by removing the hands and finding how the dial is removed. Then you take out the escapement. Now you start taking off each bridge and the gears and other pieces connected to them.

The Inside of a Mechanical Watch

Eventually you get to the barrel which houses the mainspring. The mainspring is the power source of the watch. When you twist the crown, you are winding the mainspring.

The Second to Last Step

 Once you have taken it all apart you can put it into an ultrasonic cleaner or in a special watch cleaning machine which spins the parts in a cleaning solution.

The Final Step

 Now you can put the watch back together. Just go in reverse order and you should be fine. Be sure to put lubricrants in areas with high amounts of friction. Certain oils with varying lubricating properties should be used for different parts.
